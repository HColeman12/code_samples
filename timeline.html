Space Race Timeline

Sputnik 1

October 4, 1957

The U.S.S.R. launches the first artificial satellite into orbit, catching the Americans by surprise and launching the space race.

Laika

November 3, 1957

The U.S.S.R. launches Sputnik 2. Onboard is a stray dog, Laika, the first living creature to be launched into orbit. There was never a plan to attempt recovery, and Laika dies after a few hours.

First Human to Orbit Earth

April 12, 1961

The U.S.S.R. launched the first human into orbit, Yuri Gagarin, on Vostok 1.

First American in Space

May 5, 1961

Alan B. Shepard became the first American in space during a suborbital flight aboard his Mercury capsule named Freedom 7.

J.F.K. Sets Nation's Goal

May 25, 1961

President John F. Kennedy announced before a special joint session of Congress the ambitious goal of sending an American safely to the Moon before the end of the decade.

First American in Orbit

February 20, 1962

John Glenn becomes the first American to orbit the Earth aboard the Friendship 7.

First Woman in Space

June 16, 1963

Soviet cosmonaut Valentina Tereshkova becomes the first woman in space. She spends more than 70 hours in orbit.

First Space Walk

March 18, 1965

The first space walk is performed by Soviet cosmonaut Aleksei Leonov.

First American Space Walk

June 3, 1965

The first American space walk was completed by Ed White on Gemini 4.

First Docking in Space

March 16, 1966

Neil Armstrong and David Scott on Gemini 8 docked with an Agena target.

Apollo 1 Fire

January 27, 1967

Astronauts Virgil Grissom, Ed White, and Roger Chaffee were killed in a fire during a launchpad test.

First Spaceflight Fatality

April 23, 1967

Cosmonaut Vladimir Komarov, on Soyuz 1, became the first spaceflight fatality.

First Crewed Flight to the Moon

December 24, 1968

Frank Borman, James Lovell, and William Anders orbit the Moon on Apollo 8.

Man Walks
on the Moon

July 20, 1969

Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walk on the Moon while Michael Collins orbits in the command module, Columbia.
